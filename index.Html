The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation's trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in
renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

"Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a renowned economist and social entrepreneur, served as the Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh in 2007-2008. Appointed during a period of political instability,
he led a caretaker government, aiming to restore order and hold fair elections. Yunus is best known for founding Grameen Bank and pioneering the microcredit movement, which empowers the poor,
particularly women, by providing small loans without collateral. His work in poverty alleviation and social business earned him the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006. Yunus remains a prominent
advocate for using business to address social issues."
